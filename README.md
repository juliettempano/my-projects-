## **Identifying patient factors associated with higher pmdd severity to support early intervention strategies
** ##

**Project Overview**

This project explores various factors such as stress levels, lifestyle factors, age groups, and symptom severity in women’s health dataset 

**Objectives**

- Analyze symptom severity trends
- Explore age-based differences
- Investigate relationships between stress and lifestyle factors
- Practice SQL-based exploratory analysis

**Tools Used**

- SQL
- Excel
- Git/GitHub

**Key Analyses**

- Stress levels vs average symptom severity
- Lifestyle factors by age group
- Treatment history comparisons
- PMDD subgroup analysis
  
**Key Findings**
Analysis of patient demograohic, lifestyle and clinical variable identified a consistent pattern across the dataset. Women aged 21-25 reported the highest average PMDD symptom severity across multiple analyses. 

Supporting observations included:
- Higher symptom severity across lifestyle categories (healthy, unhealthy and moderate)
- Higher symotom severity regardless of treatment history (Medication, Therapy, None)
- Greater symptom severity across menstrual cycle regularity groups (irregular,regular)

If validated using a larger patient population, these findings could help women's health organizations priortize screening and early intervention strategies for younger patients (21-25yr old) who appear to be at greater risk of PMDD symptoms. 

**Recommendation**
Based on the observed patterns, women's health organization should consider evaluation whether patients aged 21-25 benefit from enhanced PMDD screening, symptom tracking, or earlier clinical intervention. While these findings require validation on larger datasets, this explaratory analysis highlights a potentially high-risk patient segment worthy of further investigation


**Limitations**
This analysis is explaratory and based on a relatively small observational dataset. While consistent trends were observed, the findings do not establish causation. Larger datasets and statistical significance testing would be required to inform clinical decision-making. 


**Future Improvements**

- Perform hypothesis testing (ANOVA or t-tests) to determine whether difference between age groups are statisically      significant
- Build a predictive model to identify patients at risk for severe PMDD symptoms
- Examine interactions between lifestyle, stress, and treatment history using regression analysis
- Validate findings using larger clinical datasets
- Incorporate additional demographic and behavioral variables to better understand drivers of symptom severity
